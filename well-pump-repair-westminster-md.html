Types of Well Pumps We Service

Submersible Pumps

Most common for deep wells (25-300 feet). Pump and motor are sealed and installed directly in the well. Highly efficient, provide consistent pressure, and are very reliable. Brands we install: Goulds, Grundfos, Franklin Electric.

Jet Pumps

Above-ground pumps for shallow wells (up to 25 feet). Use suction to draw water. Easier to service, cost-effective, and work well for many Westminster homeowners with shallow wells.

VFD (Variable Frequency Drive) Systems

Smart pumps that adjust speed based on demand, providing constant pressure, energy savings, and extended pump life. Perfect for homes with pressure fluctuations or high water demand.

Signs Your Well Pump Needs Service

  • No water at all – Pump not running, check power, pressure switch, or pump failure
  • Pressure fluctuates or drops – Worn pump, failing pressure switch, waterlogged tank, or dropping water table
  • Pump runs continuously – Could be leak in drop pipe, bad check valve, or failing pressure switch. Shut off immediately to avoid burning out motor.
  • Air sputtering from faucets – Drop pipe leak, faulty check valve, or low water table
  • Water looks dirty or sandy – Worn pump impeller, sand entering well, or casing breach
  • High electric bills – Pump working too hard due to worn components or improper sizing

If you notice any of these, call us immediately for diagnosis. Early intervention can prevent complete pump failure and costly emergency replacement.

Pressure Tank & System Sizing

Proper pressure tank sizing is critical to prevent pump short cycling (frequent on/off) which wears out the pump prematurely. We calculate required tank drawdown based on your pump's flow rate and desired cycle time (typically 1-2 minutes between cycles).

General guidelines:

  • 1-2 bathrooms: 20-gallon tank
  • 3-4 bathrooms: 30-50 gallon tank
  • Larger homes or high demand: 60-80 gallon tank

We ensure your tank and pump are properly matched for longevity and consistent pressure.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does well pump installation cost in Westminster MD?
Cost varies by well depth, pump type, and system complexity. Typical ranges: Submersible pump $1,500-4,000; Jet pump $800-2,000; VFD systems $3,000-6,000+. Includes pump, tank, electrical, permits, and drilling coordination if needed. We provide free, itemized quotes.
How long do well pumps last?
Submersible pumps: 8-15 years; Jet pumps: 10-20 years; VFD systems: 12-20 years. Factors: water quality (sand, sediment), usage patterns, proper sizing, voltage stability, and maintenance. Annual check-ups extend lifespan.
What size pressure tank do I need?
Tank size depends on pump capacity (GPM) and desired drawdown time. Goal: 1-2 minutes of water between pump cycles. Undersized tanks cause short cycling and premature pump failure. We calculate exact requirements during consultation.
Can I replace my well pump myself?
DIY replacement is not recommended and often requires permits. Proper installation needs specialized equipment (hoisting/lowering pump), electrical licensing, understanding of well construction, and proper testing. Mistakes can contaminate water, damage well casing, create safety hazards, and void warranties. Professional installation ensures safe, code-compliant results.
What causes low water pressure in a well system?
Causes include: undersized pressure tank, failing pressure switch, waterlogged tank (low air charge), clogged sediment filter, failing pump (worn impellers, weakened motor), partially failed check valve, incorrect pressure settings, or dropping water table. We diagnose systematically to identify the root cause.
