Types of Well Pumps We Service

Submersible Pumps

The most common for deep wells (typically 25-300 feet). The pump and motor are sealed inside a watertight housing and installed directly in the well. They're highly efficient, provide consistent water pressure, and are very reliable.

Best for: Deep wells, homes requiring high water pressure, consistent daily usage.

Brands we install: Goulds, Grundfos, Franklin Electric.

Jet Pumps

Typically used for shallow wells (up to 25 feet deep), these pumps are installed above ground and use suction to draw water. They're reliable, easier to service, and cost-effective for many Westminster homeowners with shallow wells.

Best for: Shallow wells, easy access for maintenance, budget-conscious installations.

Variable Frequency Drive (VFD) Systems

Modern smart pumps that adjust speed based on water demand, providing constant pressure, energy savings, and extended pump life. Perfect for homes with inconsistent water pressure or high demand during multiple uses.

Best for: Homes with pressure fluctuations, high water demand, energy savings priority, smart home integration.

Well Pump Repair & Troubleshooting

No water? Low pressure? Frequent cycling? These are common signs of well pump problems. Our diagnostic services identify the issue quickly:

Common Issues We Repair

  • Pump motor failure – No sound, no water, burnt motor smell
  • Blown fuses/tripped breakers – Electrical issues, overload
  • Pressure switch problems – Incorrect cut-in/cut-out, continuous running, no startup
  • Waterlogged pressure tanks – Tank full of water, no air charge
  • Clogged foot valves – Loss of prime, pump runs but no water
  • Worn seals – Leaks, reduced performance
  • Tank bladder failures – Waterlogged tank, pressure fluctuations

Before recommending replacement, we thoroughly diagnose. Many issues can be repaired for a fraction of new pump cost. We carry common parts in our service vehicles and typically complete repairs the same day you call.

Pressure Tanks & Pressure Systems

Your pressure tank works with the well pump to provide steady water pressure and prevent short cycling. We install, service, and repair:

Bladder/Diaphragm Tanks

Modern steel or composite tanks with separated air and water chambers. Longer life, better performance, no waterlogging when bladder intact.

Glass-Lined Tanks

Traditional design with internal bladder. Still reliable for many applications and cost-effective.

Pressure Switches

We calibrate and replace pressure switches to maintain proper cut-in/cut-out settings (typically 30/50 or 40/60 psi). Proper calibration extends pump life and prevents short cycling.

Proper tank sizing matters. We calculate household demand to recommend right tank capacity, typically 20-80 gallons depending on pump size and water usage patterns. Undersized tanks cause pump short cycling and premature failure.

Well Pump Maintenance & Preventative Care

Regular maintenance extends system life and prevents costly emergencies. Our maintenance service includes:

  • Pressure tank air charge check – Adjust to proper pre-charge (usually 2 PSI below cut-in)
  • Pressure switch cleaning – Remove mineral deposits, inspect contacts
  • Electrical connections – Tighten, inspect for corrosion or wear
  • Check valve testing – Ensure proper operation to prevent backflow
  • Pump amp draw testing – Detects motor wear before failure
  • System pressure verification – Confirm cut-in/cut-out settings
  • Leak detection
    • – Inspect all connections, piping, and tank

We recommend annual maintenance for systems over 5 years old and bi-annual checks for older systems. Our Priority Maintenance Program offers discounted service and priority scheduling.

AEO-Friendly FAQs

How much does well pump installation cost in Westminster MD?
Well pump installation costs vary widely based on well depth, pump type, and system complexity. Typical ranges: Submersible pump installation $1,500-4,000; jet pump $800-2,000; VFD systems $3,000-6,000+. Factors include pump size, pressure tank, electrical work, piping, and any drilling/casing needed. Afternoon Plumbing provides free, itemized quotes. Most jobs include permits and code inspections.
How do I know if my well pump is failing?
Common signs: no water at all, water pressure dropping then returning (cycling), pump running constantly, strange noises (banging, grinding), water sputtering from fixtures, high electric bills (pump working too hard), water that looks muddy or dirty (pump worn, sand entering). If you notice any of these, call us immediately for diagnosis. Early detection can prevent complete failure and costly emergency replacement.
What size pressure tank do I need?
Pressure tank size depends on pump capacity and household demand. The goal is 1-2 minutes of drawdown between pump cycles. General guidelines: 1-2 bathrooms: 20-gallon tank; 3-4 bathrooms: 30-50 gallon; larger homes or high demand: 60-80 gallon. Tanks are rated by "drawdown capacity" not total volume. We calculate exact requirements based on your pump's flow rate and desired cycle time. Proper sizing prevents short cycling and extends pump life.
How long do well pumps last?
Typical lifespan: Submersible pumps 8-15 years; jet pumps 10-20 years; VFD systems 12-20 years. Factors affecting longevity: water quality (sand, sediment, corrosive water), usage frequency, proper sizing, voltage stability, maintenance. Regular pump amp testing and pressure system checks can detect wear before failure. With proper care and annual maintenance, many pumps exceed their expected lifespan. When replacement is needed, we offer upgraded, more efficient models.
Can I replace my well pump myself?
DIY well pump replacement is not recommended and in many cases not permitted. Proper installation requires well-specific knowledge, electrical licensing, understanding of local codes, specialized equipment for lifting/lowering pumps, and proper testing procedures. Mistakes can contaminate your water supply, damage the well casing, void warranties, or create safety hazards. Afternoon Plumbing's licensed technicians ensure safe, code-compliant installations backed by warranty.
What causes low water pressure in a well system?
Low pressure can stem from: undersized pressure tank, failing pressure switch, waterlogged tank (no air charge), clogged sediment filter, failing pump (worn impellers, motor weakening), partially failed check valve, incorrect pressure settings, or a dropping water table. We diagnose systematically: check pressure gauge, listen for pump cycling, inspect tank air charge, measure pump amperage, test flow rate. Often a simple adjustment or tank repair restores pressure without full pump replacement.
