When it's time to replace

Signs your water heater is on its last legs

  • Water is never hot enough—or runs cold after a few minutes
  • Rust-colored water or a rotten-egg smell
  • Visible leaks or puddles under the tank
  • Strange noises (popping, rumbling, knocking)
  • Age—most tanks last 8–12 years

Tank vs. tankless

Which type is right for your home?

  • Tank (40–80 gallons): Lower upfront cost, familiar tech, steady hot water for multiple fixtures.
  • Tankless (on-demand): Instant hot water, unlimited supply, 50% longer lifespan, smaller footprint.

We'll size it correctly and explain which makes more sense for your household and budget.

Real-world example

A standard 50-gallon electric unit (most common for homes without gas) typically installs for around $1,650 — including the tank, removal of the old unit, and code-compliant installation. Gas units run slightly higher due to venting and gas line work.
