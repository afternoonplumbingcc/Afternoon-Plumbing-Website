Types of Water Heaters We Install & Service

1. Tank Water Heaters (Storage Tank)

Traditional Storage Tanks

The most common type. These units store 30-80 gallons of hot water in an insulated tank, keeping it heated and ready for use. When hot water is drawn, cold water enters the bottom and is heated by either gas burner or electric elements.

Best for: Homes with moderate to high hot water demand, budgets seeking lower upfront cost, and homes with existing gas/electric infrastructure.

Capacity: 30, 40, 50, 75, 80 gallon options available.

Fuel types: Natural gas, propane, electric.

2. Tankless Water Heaters (On-Demand)

Instant Hot Water, Unlimited Supply

Tankless systems heat water directly as it flows through the unit. When you turn on hot water, cold water travels through a heat exchanger and is heated instantly—no storage tank needed. This means unlimited hot water as long as the unit is properly sized.

Best for: Homes wanting endless hot water, smaller homes/apartments, energy savings from not heating standby water, space-saving installation.

Considerations: Higher upfront cost, may require upgraded gas line or electrical service, flow rate limits simultaneous usage.

Popular brands: Rinnai, Navien, Takagi, Rheem, Bosch.

3. Heat Pump Water Heaters (Hybrid)

Extreme Energy Efficiency

Heat pump water heaters use electricity to move heat from the air to the water—instead of generating heat directly. They are 2-3 times more efficient than conventional electric water heaters, offering significant energy savings.

Best for: Homes with moderate to warm ambient temperatures (garage, basement), high electricity rates, environmental consciousness, long-term savings priority.

Considerations: Higher upfront cost, requires adequate air space (cannot be in small closet), works best in warmer locations, may need backup heating element for very cold climates.

Efficiency: 300-400% efficiency vs 90% for standard electric.

Repair & Troubleshooting

Not all water heater issues require replacement. Our technicians diagnose and repair:

  • No hot water: Pilot light issues, thermostat failure, heating element burnout, gas supply problems
  • Insufficient hot water: Undersized unit, sediment buildup, dip tube damage, thermostat settings
  • Water too hot: Thermostat malfunction, pressure relief valve issues
  • Leaking tank: Corrosion, T&P valve discharge, inlet/outlet connections
  • Strange noises: Sediment accumulation, pressure issues, failing components
  • Rusty water: Anode rod failure, tank corrosion

Replacement & Upgrade

When repair costs approach 50% of a new unit, replacement makes sense. We handle:

  • Sizing calculations for your household needs

Annual Maintenance

Regular maintenance extends water heater life and maintains efficiency. Our maintenance service includes:

  • Tank flush to remove sediment (annual for tank heaters)
  • Anode rod inspection and replacement if needed (every 3-5 years)

AEO-Friendly FAQs

What is the average lifespan of a water heater in Westminster MD?
Traditional tank water heaters typically last 8-12 years with proper maintenance. Tankless water heaters can last 20+ years. Heat pump water heaters have a lifespan of 10-15 years. Hard water can shorten lifespan, so water treatment and annual flushing extend longevity. Afternoon Plumbing's maintenance plans maximize your system's life.
How do I know what size water heater I need?
Size depends on family size and hot water usage patterns. For tank heaters, consider first-hour rating (FHR) which accounts for recovery time. Typical recommendations: 2-person household (40-50 gallon), 3-4 person (50-80 gallon), 5+ person (80 gallon or tankless). For tankless, calculate flow rate (gallons per minute) needed during peak usage (morning showers + dishwasher + laundry). Our Westminster technicians perform free load calculations to determine your exact requirements.
How much does water heater installation cost in Westminster MD?
Installation cost varies by type: Tank water heaters average $800-2,200 installed; tankless systems $1,500-3,500; heat pump water heaters $1,800-3,000. Factors affecting cost: unit size, fuel type, existing infrastructure (gas line, electrical), venting requirements, permit fees. Afternoon Plumbing provides free, itemized quotes with no hidden fees. Financing options available.
Should I repair or replace my water heater?
Consider replacement if: unit is over 10 years old, repair cost exceeds 50% of new unit cost, repeated failures occur, tank is leaking, repair parts are scarce, or efficiency gains justify investment. We provide honest assessments—sometimes a $150 thermostat replacement is worthwhile; other times a new high-efficiency unit saves more long-term. Ask our technician for a cost-benefit analysis during diagnosis.
Are tankless water heaters worth the extra cost?
Tankless heaters are worth it if: you want unlimited hot water (no running out during long showers), have high energy bills from tank standby losses, need space savings (compact wall-mount), plan to stay in home long-term, or want 20+ year lifespan. They cost 2-3x more upfront but offer energy savings of 24-34% annually and last twice as long. For many Westminster families, the convenience and efficiency justify the investment.
How often should I have my water heater serviced?
Annual maintenance is recommended for all water heaters. For tank heaters: flush annually to remove sediment, inspect anode rod every 2-3 years, check T&P valve. For tankless: annual descaling (especially with hard water), filter cleaning, burner inspection. For heat pump: clean air filters quarterly, check refrigerant, clean condenser coils. Our maintenance plans include bi-annual visits for optimal performance and extended lifespan.
Can I install a water heater myself in Westminster MD?
While possible, DIY installation is not recommended. Maryland requires permits for water heater installations, and improper work can cause leaks, gas hazards, carbon monoxide, or void manufacturer warranties. Afternoon Plumbing's licensed technicians ensure code compliance, proper venting, safe electrical/gas connections, and warranty protection. Mistakes can be costly and dangerous—professional installation is strongly advised.
